About the Team

Interstellar Rocketry is a student-run team focused on safe, ambitious rocketry. We combine engineering rigor with hands-on building to bring big ideas off the drawing board and into the sky.

Our members span mechanical, electrical, computer science, and physics backgrounds. We design every subsystem in-house: structures, propulsion integration, avionics, recovery, and ground support.

Each season, we pursue a flagship mission, develop experimental payloads, and mentor new members through a structured onboarding program that covers rocketry fundamentals and shop safety.

  • Mission: Build reliable rockets that reach new altitudes while staying within strict safety standards.
  • Focus Areas: Airframe design, propulsion integration, flight computers, telemetry, and recovery systems.
  • Where We Fly: Regional high-power ranges and national student competitions.
  • Who Can Join: All majors and experience levels—no prior rocketry experience required.

Team Structure

We organize into subteams so everyone can specialize while still understanding the full vehicle. Leaders coordinate design reviews and integration between subsystems.

Mission Control

Leadership & Systems Engineering

Sets mission goals, manages timelines, and ensures each subsystem integrates into a reliable flight vehicle.

Example roles: project lead, safety officer, operations lead.

Propulsion & Airframe

Structures & Flight Dynamics

Designs the rocket airframe, motor mount, and recovery hardware, and runs simulations to predict thrust, stability, and altitude.

Tools: CAD, FEA, OpenRocket.

Avionics

Electronics & Software

Builds the flight computer stack: altimeters, sensors, GPS, and telemetry links. Develops firmware and flight-data tools.

Stack: microcontrollers, embedded C, Python, RF.

Outreach

Media & Education

Shares our work at events, manages social media, and runs workshops that introduce new members to rocketry.

Focus: STEM demos, design nights, launch recaps.
